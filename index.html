Eclipse

"Eclipse" refers to the partial or complete blockage of one astronomical object's light by another. When the Earth, Moon, and Sun are lined up, an eclipse of the Sun or Moon takes place. The Greek word "ἔκλειψις, ekleípō" that gives us the term "eclipse" means "abandonment." The Greeks thought of a solar eclipse as the Sun leaving Earth.
